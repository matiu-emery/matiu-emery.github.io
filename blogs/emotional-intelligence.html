What is Emotional intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence, commonly referred to as EQ, is the ability to recognize, understand, and manage one's own emotions and the emotions of others. It involves the ability to perceive emotions, to access and generate emotions so as to assist thought, to understand emotions and emotional knowledge, and to reflectively regulate emotions so as to promote emotional and intellectual growth.

EQ is important because it enables individuals to understand and manage their own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. This allows them to communicate effectively, build strong relationships, and make sound decisions. EQ also plays a crucial role in leadership and teamwork, as it helps individuals to motivate and inspire others, resolve conflicts, and create a positive work environment.

What is intelligence quotient?

IQ, or Intelligence Quotient, is a measure of cognitive abilities and potential. It is determined by a standardized test that assesses abilities such as reasoning, problem-solving, and memory. IQ is often used as an indicator of academic and professional success.

What is the difference?

The difference between EQ and IQ is that EQ is a measure of emotional intelligence, while IQ is a measure of cognitive intelligence. Emotional intelligence refers to the ability to recognize, understand, and manage one's own emotions and the emotions of others. It includes skills such as empathy, self-awareness, and self-regulation. IQ, on the other hand, is a measure of cognitive abilities and potential, such as reasoning, problem-solving, and memory.
